PACTA (Paris Agreement Capital Transition Assessment) is a comprehensive tool designed to help investors assess the alignment of their portfolios with global climate goals. By evaluating how investee companies are positioned relative to different climate scenarios, PACTA provides portfolio insights into the potential risks and opportunities of transitioning to a low-carbon economy. PACTA uses climate scenario analysis to assess the exposure of portfolios to carbon-intensive sectors and technologies, helping investors understand how well their holdings align with pathways to limit global warming to well below 2°C, with an aspirational target of 1.5°C. The tool evaluates various asset classes, including listed equities and corporate bonds, and applies sector-specific metrics to assess the emissions intensity and transition progress of companies.

The results generated by PACTA allow users to get a granular view of the exposure and climate goal alignment of their portfolios by sector and for related technologies. This information can be used to:
  • help steer investment decisions in line with climate scenarios;
  • inform decisions in relation to climate target-setting;
  • gain insights that can support engagement with clients on their respective climate actions;
  • identify exposure to transition risks associated with a shift to a low-carbon economy.
